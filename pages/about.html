Meet Adrienne

“Hi, I'm Adrienne,

I strive to provide high-quality therapeutic massage and bodywork to clients in order to help them heal, recover, rejuvenate, and maintain overall healthy physical and mental wellness. I strive to create quality relationships with each client and provide superior service while exceeding expectations.

My goal is simple: To help you help yourself. Whether you suffer from the aches and pains of daily life, an injury incurred from a slip on the ice, the repercussions of a car accident, or overall chronic pain, I can work through the puzzle of soft tissue issues to help you effectively find relief.

Not everyone functions the same. Therefore, every massage shouldn’t be the same. I customize each session to fit your individual needs and goals, whether we’re working over the long term or fixing the issue of the day.”

Growing up in the suburbs of Minneapolis, I was very active in alpine ski racing, volleyball, and dance. All that got halted for a moment when I was in three car accidents when I was 16. I was able to walk out of all of them, assuming I was “fine,” but as time passed, I learned first-hand what whiplash can do to your back, neck, and head (and sometimes, your spirit).
I didn’t want to let it slow me down, so I didn’t. It took a few years, but I discovered a combination of massage therapy, chiropractic care, and a routine of Pilates, running, and other exercise helped me manage the symptoms and get back to living life as usual.

After graduating from the University of Denver as a young adult, I taught children’s alpine skiing in Keystone, Colorado for four years, and also spent a summer living, working, and playing outside Denali National Park in Alaska. I didn’t let the biggest mountains I could find slow me down, even after a few bumps and bruises during my teen years.

In 2016, I graduated from CenterPoint Massage and Shiatsu Therapy School and Clinic with a specialization in Western Therapeutic Massage.
Today, I specialize in helping you find relief from upper body aches and pains; whether you suffer from chronic conditions, whiplash, tension headaches, old or new injury, or just the stress of daily life, I can help you find relief so you can get back to climbing mountains (or life as usual).

Outside of work, I like adventuring with my husband, Dan, whether it be out of state, out of country, or through the northwoods of Minnesota with our German Shorthair Pointer/Springer Spaniel mix, Rufus. We love kayaking, paddleboarding, hiking, fishing, camping, skiing (cross-country and downhill), snowshoeing… really anything that gets us out enjoying mother nature.
